part 01 · scraping

pull the post archive.

get the writer's last 30–100 linkedin posts into one paste-able block. two paths: cheap and manual, or fast and scripted.

path a · manual

copy-paste (5 minutes)

go to the target profile → click "see all activity" → click "posts" → scroll until you see 30–50 posts → copy each post text into a google doc or txt file.

format each entry with the date and engagement numbers. engagement is how the voice prompt distinguishes signal from noise.

$0 · recommended for first run
path b · api

reversecontact (power users)

sign up at app.reversecontact.com, grab an api key. pull profile (1 credit), fire posts request with a webhook token (2 credits), collect the payload.

returns up to 100 posts with full engagement data. total cost per exec: 3 credits, ~$0.30.

best for 10+ execs

format for the paste block (path a)

--- POST 1 (2026-03-14 — 47 reactions, 12 comments, 3 reposts) [post text here] --- POST 2 (2026-03-12 — 83 reactions, 5 comments, 1 repost) [post text here]

api workflow (path b)

step 1 · pull the profile (1 credit)

curl -s -X POST 'https://api.reversecontact.com/v2/fetch/persons' \ -H 'Content-Type: application/json' \ -H "Authorization: Bearer YOUR_API_KEY" \ -d '{"url": "https://www.linkedin.com/in/USERNAME"}'

step 2 · create a webhook token

curl -s -X POST 'https://webhook.site/token'

step 3 · fire the posts request (2 credits)

curl -s -X POST 'https://api.reversecontact.com/v2/fetch/persons/posts/live' \ -H 'Content-Type: application/json' \ -H "Authorization: Bearer YOUR_API_KEY" \ -d '{ "url": "https://www.linkedin.com/in/USERNAME", "webhookUrl": "https://webhook.site/YOUR_UUID" }'

step 4 · wait 20s, then collect

curl -s "https://webhook.site/token/YOUR_UUID/requests?sorting=newest&per_page=1"

save the content as posts.json. you now have up to 100 posts with full engagement data.

part 02 · voice fingerprint

the wow prompt.

paste this into claude or chatgpt along with the post archive. outputs a 9-section structured voice fingerprint that replaces generic "be warm and professional" guidance with explicit, data-driven rules any ghostwriter can follow.

you are a voice analyst. your job is to produce a structured "voice fingerprint" of a linkedin writer based on a corpus of their posts. the output replaces generic "be warm and professional" style guidance with explicit, data-driven rules that an ai ghostwriter can follow. input: i will paste 30-100 linkedin posts below this prompt, each with date and engagement numbers where available. output: a complete voice fingerprint document with these 9 sections in this exact order. do not skip sections. do not invent examples. every claim must cite a real verbatim line from the corpus. --- ## 1. core identity 1 paragraph, max 4 sentences. career arc, current role, credibility anchor. ## 2. voice archetype one line in "the [adjective] [noun]" format (e.g., "the transparent operator", "the reluctant expert"). 2-3 sentence rationale grounded in the corpus. ## 3. style fingerprint ### 3a. sentence mechanics - avg sentence length (measure it — count words across 30+ sentences) - length variance: consistent / mixed / extreme - fragment frequency: 0-10 score based on % of sentences <=3 words - pov: first-person singular / plural / you-voice / mix - verb patterns: active vs passive tilt, present vs past tense tendency cite 2-3 verbatim sentences that exemplify the rhythm. ### 3b. paragraph rhythm - avg paragraph length (in sentences) - line-break pattern: one-per-line / clustered / mixed - whitespace density: how often blank lines appear - visual rhythm: describe it ("staircase down", "dense blocks", "haiku beats") include one real 3-4 paragraph block as an example. ### 3c. opening patterns (find 3-5) each pattern needs: - a short label - when the writer uses it (what triggers this opener) - one verbatim real example from the corpus ### 3d. closing patterns (find 3-5) same structure as openers. each closer must tag which content-type mode(s) it fits (personal story / thought leadership / product launch / etc). ### 3e. connectors & transitions list 5-10 real connector phrases the writer uses ("turns out,", "here's the thing:", "so.", "which is when i realized..."). note whether they favor short pivots or narrative transitions. ### 3f. signature punctuation & formatting - em-dash usage (count appearances across the corpus — common or rare?) - ellipsis usage - emoji: which ones, how often, position (inline / end) - caps for emphasis - bullet or arrow usage, when - hashtag frequency and style - any unusual typographic signatures (lowercase "i", etc.) ### 3g. anti-slop overrides some patterns that are generally "ai slop" are actually signature for this specific writer. identify them. for each, tag as: - OVERRIDE (pattern is baseline style — ignore the generic ban) - PARTIAL OVERRIDE (specific instances allowed, others banned — name which) - KEEP-VERBATIM SIGNATURE LINE (one exact passage is exempt, even from hard rules) if no overrides exist, write "no overrides — standard anti-slop rules apply". ## 4. vocabulary **favor list** — words and phrases the writer actually uses. ideally with counts from the corpus. 10-20 items. **signature lines** — load-bearing thesis or brand lines that recur across posts. these are the lines that make a reader think "that's them". 3-5 items. **avoid list** — words that never appear in the corpus or are known llm slop this writer would never use. 10-15 items. ## 5. content-type modes classify the posts into the modes this person actually writes in. for each mode, document: - trigger (when this mode activates) - structural shift from the base fingerprint (what changes vs sections 3a-3f) - 2 verbatim real examples with engagement numbers - warnings (when this mode flops — cite a real low-performing post if possible) common modes to look for: personal story / thought leadership / product launch / build-in-public / contrarian take / framework post / reaction or commentary / celebration. include only modes the writer actually uses. ## 6. do / don't hard rules. concise. 5-10 items each side. ## 7. tone markers adjective list that captures the feel. 5-8 items. ## 8. performance snapshot stats from the corpus: - avg reactions (excluding viral outliers) - top post and what made it work - worst format (format that correlates with lowest engagement) - posting cadence - language splits if multilingual ## 9. signature patterns to preserve a 3-5 item bulleted summary of the most distinctive things about this voice that must never be lost in ghostwritten content. --- rules for your output: - no invented examples. every quote must come from the provided corpus. - use numbers, not adjectives. "avg 11 words/sentence, extreme variance" beats "punchy and varied". - if you can't find evidence for a claim, leave it out. - treat this as a reference document, not an essay. write for the ghostwriter that will consume it. now process the following corpus: [PASTE POSTS HERE]

part 03 · moments bank

mine the lived stories.

voice tells you how they write. moments tell you what they've lived. this prompt mines 15–25 specific, usable stories from the same post archive. raw material for every future post. no more inventing generic anecdotes.

you are mining a linkedin post archive for specific lived moments that can be reused as anchors for future content. input: the same corpus you just analyzed in part 2, or a fresh paste. output: a structured "moments bank" with 15-25 entries. each entry is a specific, usable story — not an opinion, not a framework, not generic advice. --- ## what counts as a moment a moment has: - a specific time (date, era, or "day before X") - a specific place or setting - at least one named person or concrete detail (a number, a quote, an object) - a shift or realization that resulted from it things that are NOT moments: - abstract claims ("i learned the importance of persistence") - advice without a story ("always follow up with prospects") - product positioning or feature descriptions - generic gratitude posts ## how to find moments in the corpus scan for posts that open with: - "i remember..." - "last week..." - "on the day..." - "when i was..." - a specific time stamp (e.g., "at 8:32am saturday morning") - a named person in the first 2 sentences also scan for posts with specific numbers ($X, X%), named companies, or concrete sensory details (what was in the room, what was said). ## output format group entries by category. use only the categories that apply: - career (first job, pivot, exit, hiring, firing, quitting) - customer (sales calls, deals won/lost, support moments) - product (launch, bug, customer-use case, design decision) - team (cofounder disagreement, hire, loss of teammate) - personal (only what the writer has already made public) - industry (conference, talk, peer interaction, public debate) - founding (origin story, early survival moment) each entry uses this exact format: ### <short label> - **when:** <approximate date or era> - **category:** <one of the above> - **setting:** <1 line — where, what was going on> - **what happened:** <2-4 sentences with concrete details, names when safe> - **what shifted:** <1-2 sentences on the realization or outcome> - **details worth keeping:** - <exact numbers, names, quotes, dates> - <before/after facts> - **themes:** <tags for what concepts this moment illustrates> - **safe to use:** <public | with-edits | consent-needed> - **source post:** <approx date or identifier of the post it came from> ## hygiene rules - no inventing. every detail must come from the corpus. - if a moment is mentioned but thin, mark it "with-edits" and flag what's missing. - if a moment involves a named third party in a negative light, mark it "consent-needed". - target 15-25 entries. quality over quantity. if the corpus only yields 10 genuine moments, deliver 10 and note why. now process the following corpus: [PASTE POSTS HERE]

what this unlocks. most execs have years of posts full of specific stories they've told once and forgotten. the moments bank catalogues them. next time someone says "write a post about customer-centricity" you feed in the bank and the ghostwriter finds the actual sales call from 2019 where you lost a deal because you misjudged what the cfo cared about. that's the difference between "a post about customer-centricity" and "a post only you could have written".

part 04 · scoring formula

two axes, 1–10 each.

calculated purely from the raw data. no subjective judgment. consistency (how reliably they post) and quality (performance vs benchmark).

axis 2 · quality

benchmark: 0.5% engagement rate (defensible floor for linkedin personal accounts with 10k+ followers).

engagement_rate = (reactions + comments*3 + reposts*2) / followers

comments are 3x because they signal deeper resonance. reposts are 2x because they extend reach. reactions are baseline.

window selection: 90d if 10+ posts, else widen to 180d, else 360d. take the median engagement rate across the window (not the mean, mean gets skewed by viral outliers). divide by 0.005 to get the multiplier vs benchmark.

why this matters

plot consistency on the x-axis and quality on the y-axis. you get a 2x2.

the silent expert

high quality, low consistency. posts well when they post. doesn't post. pure volume play.

the content machine

low quality, high consistency. posts a lot but nothing lands. needs voice + format fix.

the target zone

high both. 7.5 / 7.5 is the benchmark to aim for.

reactivation candidate

low on both. start from scratch on voice and cadence.

that diagnosis takes 2 minutes once you have the scraped data. most $15k strategy engagements never produce it.

part 05 · gap analysis

what's missing?

you have the voice. you have the moments. you have the score. this prompt reviews everything and produces a prioritized action list.

you are a gap analysis agent. your job is to review a completed voice fingerprint and moments bank, identify what's missing, and produce a prioritized expansion plan. input: - the voice fingerprint (part 2 output) - the moments bank (part 3 output) - the consistency + quality scores (part 4 output) - if available: the linkedin profile data (about, experience, education, skills) output: a structured gap analysis with the 9 categories below. every gap must include a why-it-matters line and a how-to-fill-it action. --- ## category 1: origin story does the writer have a documented "why i do what i do" narrative with a specific moment? check: does the moments bank include a founding story, career pivot moment, or personal motivation with concrete details? if missing: this is usually the single highest-impact gap. personal vulnerability + specific detail is the top-performing content format across almost every exec. action: propose 2-3 specific interview questions that would surface the story. ## category 2: unnamed frameworks does the writer repeat ideas in posts without naming them? check: scan the voice fingerprint's signature lines and the moments bank's themes. look for ideas that appear 3+ times without a label. if found: suggest a name. named frameworks become recognizable ip. ## category 3: content series does the writer have a named, recurring content series? check: look for numbered posts, recurring formats, or repeating headers. if missing: suggest 2-3 series names based on their strongest track (e.g., "notes from the field", "monday memos", "things i learned from [role]"). ## category 4: language opportunity does the writer post in multiple languages? check: the corpus language analysis from part 2. if multilingual with engagement data: compare per-language engagement. if non-english outperforms, flag the asymmetry. non-english often gets 10-30x english on linkedin because of lower supply. ## category 5: cross-platform presence does the writer create content anywhere besides linkedin? check for references to: - a newsletter (substack, beehiiv, etc.) - a podcast they host or guest on - a blog (medium, personal site) - x / twitter / threads - youtube for each platform found: note the repurposing opportunity (podcast episode → 3-5 linkedin posts, newsletter → linkedin teaser, etc). ## category 6: thought leaders & influences who does the writer @-mention, quote, or repost? check the corpus for names mentioned, peers amplified, and recommendations given. why this matters: - shows voice aspiration signals - reveals positioning ambitions - identifies collaboration opportunities ## category 7: underused profile assets are there credibility signals in the profile that never appear in posts? check for: awards, certifications, patents, publications, board seats, teaching positions, past companies (especially well-known ones), languages spoken, unusual licenses or skills. each unused asset = a potential content angle. "i learned X during my time at [famous company]" posts consistently outperform generic advice. ## category 8: format gaps looking at the performance snapshot and mode classifications, what formats is the writer NOT using that they should be? check for: never uses carousels / never uses video / never uses contrarian takes / only writes thought leadership, never personal stories. ## category 9: single highest-leverage move based on everything above, what is the ONE thing this writer should change next month that would move their xy score the most? be specific. not "post more" — "shift from 2 posts/week thought-leadership to 3 posts/week mixing thought-leadership and personal story, using the origin moment from the moments bank as your first personal-story post". --- ## output format # gap analysis — [writer name] ## summary [2-3 sentences: what's strong, what's the single biggest opportunity] ## high-priority gaps (act on these first) 1. [gap] — [why it matters] — [how to fill it] 2. [gap] — [why it matters] — [how to fill it] 3. [question] — fills: [gap category] ... --- now process the inputs: VOICE FINGERPRINT: [paste here] MOMENTS BANK: [paste here] SCORES: consistency [X], quality [Y] PROFILE DATA (optional): [paste here]
